Be Prepared!

Even the small preps help a lot!

*Emergency Plan:* Develop a comprehensive emergency plan for your household or community. This plan should include evacuation routes, designated meeting points, communication methods, and emergency contacts.
*Emergency Supplies:* Prepare an emergency kit with essential supplies such as food, water, medications, first aid supplies, flashlight, batteries, and a portable radio. Ensure that these supplies are easily accessible and regularly updated.
*Stay Informed:* Stay informed about potential hazards and emergency situations by monitoring weather forecasts, news updates, and official alerts from local authorities. Sign up for emergency alerts and notifications from relevant agencies.
*Training and Education:* Take training courses in first aid, CPR, and disaster preparedness. Educate yourself and your family members about the specific risks in your area and how to respond appropriately.
*Community Engagement:* Get involved in community emergency preparedness efforts. Participate in drills, exercises, and community planning meetings. Collaborate with neighbors and local organizations to enhance resilience and support each other during emergencies.
*Financial Preparedness:* Prepare financially for emergencies by having emergency savings, insurance coverage (such as flood insurance or earthquake insurance), and important documents stored in a secure location.
